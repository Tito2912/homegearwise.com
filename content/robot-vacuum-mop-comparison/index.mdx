              <div class="card">

                  </li>

                    <strong>Noise &amp; schedules:</strong> if you run it early/late, prioritize predictable scheduling and a

        <section class="stack" id="what-to-check" aria-labelledby="what-to-check-title">
          <h2 id="what-to-check-title">How we compare robot vacuum &amp; mop models (2026)</h2>
          <div class="card">
            <p>
              Product pages often mix marketing names with bundles that include different accessories. For this guide, we
              focus on what manufacturers publish (and what’s consistent across official docs) and compare models using the
              checklist below.
            </p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Dock capabilities:</strong> emptying, water refill, mop washing, mop drying (if included).</li>
              <li><strong>Mop behavior:</strong> does it avoid rugs while mopping? does it lift the mop?</li>
              <li><strong>Consumables:</strong> filter/brush/mop replacements—availability and typical replacement cadence.</li>
              <li><strong>Maintenance access:</strong> how easy is it to remove the main brush, side brush, and rollers?</li>
              <li><strong>Floor plans:</strong> multi-floor support, room naming, zones, and “no-go” boundaries.</li>
              <li>
                <strong>Edge cleaning:</strong> check for edge strategies (or accept that corners still need manual help).
              </li>
              <li><strong>Warranty/returns:</strong> especially important for complex docks with water components.</li>
            </ul>
            <p class="muted">
              We list specs and box contents based on official manufacturer sources as of January 2026. If a spec isn’t
              clearly published, we omit it instead of guessing.
            </p>
          </div>
        </section>

        <section class="stack" id="navigation" aria-labelledby="navigation-title">
          <h2 id="navigation-title">Navigation &amp; obstacle avoidance</h2>
          <div class="card">
            <p>
              In 2026, navigation isn’t just about “mapping”—it’s about whether the robot can run reliably without
              babysitting.
            </p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Map stability:</strong> does the app keep room names/zones after updates?</li>
              <li><strong>No-go zones:</strong> can you block cords, pet bowls, and fragile areas precisely?</li>
              <li><strong>Obstacle handling:</strong> consider your reality (kids toys, socks, pet accidents).</li>
              <li><strong>Dark rooms:</strong> some systems perform differently at night—plan schedules accordingly.</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </section>

        <section class="stack" id="mopping" aria-labelledby="mopping-title">
          <h2 id="mopping-title">Mopping systems (and edge cleaning)</h2>
          <div class="card">
            <p>
              Most “vacuum + mop” combos are designed for maintenance cleaning: dust, footprints, and light spills. If you
              need more than that, the mop system matters more than raw vacuum specs.
            </p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Pad vs rotating mops:</strong> check what type of mop is used and how it’s cleaned.</li>
              <li><strong>Water control:</strong> look for adjustable water flow and “mop only” modes.</li>
              <li><strong>Rug strategy:</strong> check how the robot avoids wetting carpets (avoidance vs lifting).</li>
              <li><strong>Edges/corners:</strong> no robot hits every corner—plan a quick manual pass weekly.</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </section>

        <section class="stack" id="docks" aria-labelledby="docks-title">
          <h2 id="docks-title">Docks &amp; automation (what “hands-off” really means)</h2>
          <div class="card">
            <p>
              Docks can reduce daily work—but only if they match your routine. Before paying for “full automation”, decide
              what you’re willing to do weekly.
            </p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Auto-empty:</strong> fewer bin dumps, but you still need to clean brushes and sensors.</li>
              <li><strong>Mop wash/dry:</strong> helps reduce odor and residue if you mop often.</li>
              <li><strong>Water tanks:</strong> check refill and cleaning steps (and how often they’re needed).</li>
              <li><strong>Space:</strong> docks are bulky—measure your intended spot.</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </section>

        <section class="stack" id="maintenance" aria-labelledby="maintenance-title">
          <h2 id="maintenance-title">Maintenance &amp; consumables (the hidden “cost”)</h2>
          <div class="card">
            <p>
              Robots perform best when airflow and brushes are clean. Treat maintenance like changing a car’s oil: small,
              regular tasks beat rare deep cleans.
            </p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Weekly:</strong> wipe sensors; remove hair from brushes and rollers.</li>
              <li><strong>Monthly:</strong> wash bins and parts; deep clean brush housing; check wheels/casters.</li>
              <li><strong>Quarterly:</strong> replace filters/brushes as needed (follow your manual and usage).</li>
            </ul>
            <p class="muted">
              Want the full routine? Use our
              <a href="/blog/how-to-clean-and-maintain-robot-vacuum/">maintenance guide</a>.
            </p>
          </div>
        </section>

        <section class="stack" id="pets-carpets" aria-labelledby="pets-carpets-title">
          <h2 id="pets-carpets-title">Pets, carpets &amp; mixed floors</h2>
          <div class="card">
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Pets:</strong> prioritize easy brush cleaning and strong no-go controls near feeding areas.</li>
              <li><strong>Carpets:</strong> plan more frequent brush cleaning; avoid mopping on thick rugs.</li>
              <li><strong>Mixed floors:</strong> mapping and room-based modes matter more than anything else.</li>
              <li><strong>Thresholds:</strong> if you have high transitions, check how well the robot handles them.</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </section>

        <section class="stack" id="privacy" aria-labelledby="privacy-title">
          <h2 id="privacy-title">Apps, privacy &amp; data</h2>
          <div class="card">
            <p>
              Most robots rely on an app for mapping and schedules. Before committing, check what’s required to use core
              features.
            </p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Accounts:</strong> is an account required? is offline/basic mode possible?</li>
              <li><strong>Permissions:</strong> only grant what the robot needs (notifications, location, etc.).</li>
              <li><strong>Updates:</strong> firmware updates can change behavior—keep an eye on release notes.</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </section>

            <section class="stack" id="setup" aria-labelledby="setup-title">
              <h2 id="setup-title">Setup checklist (for better real-world results)</h2>
              <div class="card">
                <ul>
                  <li>Pick a dock spot with clearance and stable Wi‑Fi coverage.</li>
                  <li>Do one “prep pass”: remove cords, small toys, and fragile items.</li>
                  <li>Run a mapping pass, then name rooms and set no-go zones.</li>
                  <li>Start with a small schedule, then increase frequency (maintenance cleaning works best when frequent).</li>
                  <li>Keep a mini kit together: small brush, microfiber, spare filter.</li>
                </ul>
              </div>
            </section>

	        <section class="stack" id="faq" aria-labelledby="faq-title">
	          <h2 id="faq-title">FAQ</h2>
	          <div class="card">
	            <details>
	              <summary>Do robot vacuums replace a regular vacuum?</summary>
	              <div>
	                <p>
	                  Not entirely. They reduce day-to-day dust and hair, but most homes still need occasional deep vacuuming,
	                  especially on carpets and corners.
	                </p>
	              </div>
	            </details>
	            <details>
	              <summary>Is “vacuum + mop combo” enough for sticky spills?</summary>
	              <div>
	                <p>
	                  For many homes, it’s maintenance-level mopping. For sticky spills, you’ll still want occasional manual
	                  mopping or a targeted spot-clean routine.
	                </p>
	              </div>
	            </details>
	            <details>
	              <summary>How often should I replace filters and brushes?</summary>
	              <div>
	                <p>
	                  It depends on pets, hair, and frequency. Follow the manual first, then adjust based on visible buildup and
	                  airflow changes. Our maintenance guide includes a practical schedule.
	                </p>
	              </div>
	            </details>
	            <details>
	              <summary>Can I use floor cleaner or detergent in the mop tank?</summary>
	              <div>
	                <p>
	                  Only if your manufacturer explicitly approves it. Otherwise, use clean water: many detergents can foam,
	                  leave residue, or clog pathways (and some can damage seals over time).
	                </p>
	              </div>
	            </details>
	            <details>
	              <summary>How do I avoid wet rugs and carpets?</summary>
	              <div>
	                <p>
	                  Use “no-mop” zones for rug areas, or run vacuum-only on carpet rooms. If your model supports it, enable mop
	                  lifting / carpet avoidance while mopping, and start with lower water output until you’re confident in the
	                  results.
	                </p>
	              </div>
	            </details>
	            <details>
	              <summary>Is robot mopping safe for hardwood and laminate?</summary>
	              <div>
	                <p>
	                  Usually on sealed, water-safe floors. Avoid unsealed wood and water-sensitive surfaces. Keep pads clean,
	                  don’t leave wet pads sitting in place, and reduce water output if you see streaking or swelling.
	                </p>
	              </div>
	            </details>
	            <details>
	              <summary>Will it handle pet hair and long hair?</summary>
	              <div>
	                <p>
	                  Yes for many homes, but hair is the #1 reason brushes need attention. Run more frequent short cycles,
	                  remove hair from the main brush regularly, and plan for consumables like filters and pads.
	                </p>
	              </div>
	            </details>
	            <details>
	              <summary>Do I need Wi‑Fi, and what about privacy?</summary>
	              <div>
	                <p>
	                  Many robots need an app (and often an account) for mapping and schedules. If privacy matters, limit
	                  permissions, avoid voice assistants you don’t use, and consider a separate Wi‑Fi network for smart devices.
	                </p>
	              </div>
	            </details>
	            <details>
	              <summary>How often should I clean the dock and water parts?</summary>
	              <div>
	                <p>
	                  Treat it like a small appliance: quick wipe/rinse often, deeper cleaning occasionally. If you have
	                  dirty-water tanks or mop trays, empty and rinse them the same day to reduce odors.
	                </p>
	              </div>
	            </details>
	            <details>
	              <summary>Why does my robot mop smell?</summary>
	              <div>
	                <p>
	                  Odor usually comes from moisture sitting in pads, trays, or dirty-water tanks. Rinse pads, dry them fully,
	                  and keep dock trays and tanks clean.
	                </p>
	              </div>
	            </details>
