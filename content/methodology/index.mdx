    <h1>How we test &amp; evaluate products</h1>
    <p>
      The goal of our guides is to help you choose a product that fits your home and constraints (space, pets, noise tolerance, cleaning
      routine) — and to avoid surprises after purchase.
    </p>
  </section>

  <section class="stack" aria-labelledby="process">
    <h2 id="process">Our evaluation checklist</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <p>Depending on the category (robot vacuum, airfryer, coffee machine), we typically focus on:</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Performance:</strong> core job quality (cleaning / cooking / extraction), consistency, edge cases.</li>
        <li><strong>Maintenance:</strong> filters, brushes, descaling, consumables, availability of parts.</li>
        <li><strong>Usability:</strong> setup, app UX, daily workflow, error messages, accessibility.</li>
        <li><strong>Noise &amp; comfort:</strong> acceptable levels for apartments / night routines.</li>
        <li><strong>Reliability signals:</strong> recurring issues reported by users, warranty terms, known limitations.</li>
        <li><strong>Total cost:</strong> consumables + subscriptions (if any), not just sticker price.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="stack" aria-labelledby="honesty">
    <h2 id="honesty">What we do (and don’t) claim</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <p>
        We aim to be transparent about what comes from official specs, manuals, and documentation vs. what comes from aggregated feedback.
        If we don’t have high confidence, we say so.
      </p>
      <p>
        Always verify safety instructions and exact specs in the official manual — especially for electrical appliances and batteries.
      </p>
    </div>
  </section>
